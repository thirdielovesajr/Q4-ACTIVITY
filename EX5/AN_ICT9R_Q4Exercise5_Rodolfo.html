Ohm's Law

Ohm's Law describes the way current flows through a material when different levels of voltage are applied. Some materials like electrical wires present little resistance to the current flow, and this type of material is called a conductor. Hence, if this conductor is placed directly across a battery, for example, a lot of current would flow.



To help remember the formula, it is possible to use a triangle with one side horizontal and the peak at the top like a pyramid. This is sometimes known as the Ohm's Law triangle. In the top corner of the Ohm's Law triangle is the letter V, in the left-hand corner, the letter I, and in the right-hand bottom corner, R.

What makes a light bulb light up

The typical incandescent light bulb contains a thin wire (usually tungsten) called a filament that has a high electrical resistance. This filament gets very hot when an electric current passes through it. The intense temperature makes the filament glow brightly.
